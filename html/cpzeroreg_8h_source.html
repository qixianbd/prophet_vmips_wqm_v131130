00041 /* CP0 register names and masks
00042  *
00043  * A table of names for CP0's registers follows. After that follow a
00044  * series of masks by which fields of these registers can be isolated.
00045  * The masks are convenient for Boolean flags but are slightly less so
00046  * for numbers being extracted from the middle of a word because they
00047  * still need to be shifted. At least, it makes clear which field is
00048  * being accessed, and the bit numbers are clearly indicated in every mask
00049  * below.  The naming convention is as follows: Mumble is the name of some
00050  * CP0 register, Mumble_MASK is the bit mask which controls reading and
00051  * writing of the register (0 -> bit is always zero and ignores writes,
00052  * 1 -> normal read/write) and Mumble_Field_MASK is the mask used to
00053  * access the "Field" portion of register Mumble. For more information
00054  * on these fields consult "MIPS RISC Architecture", chapters 4 and 6.
00055  */
